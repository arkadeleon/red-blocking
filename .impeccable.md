## Design Context

### Users
This app is for Street Fighter III: 3rd Strike players and lab users browsing character move data on iPhone and iPad. The core jobs are to select a character, navigate move categories, inspect structured move details, and preview frame-by-frame motion with hitbox overlays.

### Brand Personality
Arcade-faithful, technical, direct. The interface should feel grounded in the original game’s character-select and move-inspection mood while still reading as a modern native SwiftUI app. It should support focused analysis rather than playful novelty.

### Aesthetic Direction
The visual direction is a dark, orange-and-coal arcade presentation inspired by the Street Fighter III: 3rd Strike selection board and legacy UI. Pixel-art avatars, layered gradients, depth, and restrained highlights are desirable. System list chrome, generic card grids, and soft default-app styling are explicitly out of character.

### Design Principles
1. Preserve the game-reference feel where it matters, especially in character selection and move presentation.
2. Keep the app analysis-first: fast scanning, clear hierarchy, and low-friction access to motion and hitbox data.
3. Adapt for iPhone and iPad without hiding core navigation or controls.
4. Accessibility improvements should strengthen clarity without flattening the intended arcade identity.
5. Prefer shared theme tokens and reusable presentation primitives over screen-specific visual one-offs.
